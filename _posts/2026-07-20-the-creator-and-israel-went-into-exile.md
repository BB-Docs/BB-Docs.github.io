### Opening

The article read at the top of the lesson quotes the Zohar's account of Israel's exile: "the Creator and Israel went into exile" together. The live question underneath the reading is what that could possibly mean — how can the Creator, who fills the whole earth, be said to go into exile at all? Rav Laitman's answer opens onto the lesson's real subject: exile and revelation are not about geography but about a person's own desires, and the only thing genuinely asked of a person along that path is intention. From there the Q&A ranges widely — into free choice, dissemination, and why a person should never look back — but keeps returning to that single point.

## Part 1 — Exile Is a Matter of Equivalence of Form

### Israel and the Nations Are Not Ethnicities

Rav Laitman opened by reframing "the Land of Israel," "Israel," and "the nations of the world" as descriptions of a person's orientation, not geography or ancestry. A person has 620 desires; where those desires point determines everything:

> "If a person's desires, his 620 desires, are aimed towards himself, they are called the nations of the world. And to the extent that he corrects them so as to bestow upon the Creator, then each and every desire turns from a Gentile to a Jew again."

### Olamot — Worlds as Degrees of Concealment

The word for "worlds," Olamot, was traced to Ha'alama (concealment) — the degree of revelation or hiddenness a person experiences is itself what a "world" is:

> "The degrees of revelation or concealment of the Creator towards the person are called Olamot, worlds, from the word Ha'alama, disappearance. According to the corrected desires, the Creator is revealed. According to still corrupted desires, the Creator is concealed."

### Why the Creator "Goes Into Exile"

Asked how the Creator could be said to go into exile at all, the answer was that this describes appearance, not location: "He simply appears to them in the form of exile... He only appears as good, that that's good" — meaning concealment itself is an act of the Creator, not an absence of Him.

## Part 2 — Intention Is the One Thing Left for the Person to Do

### The Light Does Everything Except This

Rav Laitman was direct that correcting, filling, or emptying a vessel is entirely the Light's work, not the person's:

> "The light corrects the vessels, if necessary corrupts them, corrects them, fills them, empties them... The vessel has only this point of awareness to know what state it is in and where it wants to go."

### Kavanah: Determining the Direction of Yearning

What is left for a person, then, is only to notice his current state and to yearn for the next one:

> "What is up to the person is to discover his current state that he is in each time, and from the state he is in to yearn for a state that is closer to the Creator by one degree. Meaning to determine the orientation of the desire, the orientation of the yearning. That is called intention, kavanah... So a person needs to determine only that. All else is done by the Creator."

### Gratitude for the Worst State, Not Just the Best

This intention is meant to extend even to states that feel bad, since every state — not only the pleasant ones — is what the goal requires: a person is asked to "come to a state where he is grateful for the very worst thing as he is for the best thing, because he connects to the goal."

## Part 3 — Reading Your Own State Before and After the Barrier

### Before the Barrier: Confusion Is Doing Its Job

Asked whether the states described happen before or after the Machsom (barrier), Rav Laitman located most of this discussion after it, while noting that a person before the barrier goes through corresponding, if less refined, states too:

> "Before the barrier, there aren't many clear descents. Still, we're under either double concealment or regular concealment. It is darkness."

This period, he said, can take years, and it is not wasted time — the discernments gathered there are needed later, once a person works with "the revelation of the faith above the barrier."

### Measuring a State by What Is Revealed

Asked how a person assesses which state he is in, the answer tied it directly to how much the Creator has been revealed to him at that moment — not to any external standard:

> "How do I determine where I am, how I am? It's to the extent that the Creator reveals to me... This confusion that comes each time brings more understanding later."

He illustrated this with an image of physical labor:

> "I build a house. I brought a truck full of bricks. I need to bring it up to the tenth floor. Well, it is work, but this I build. So you either look at the work being very hard, or what will come of it. If you look at the goal, then collecting those bricks, bringing them up, it's not so bad."

## Part 4 — The One Place Free Choice Exists

### Freedom in Only One Thing

Responding to a question about free choice, Rav Laitman narrowed it sharply: freedom applies only to the single relationship between a person and the Creator, nowhere else.

> "You're free only in one thing... only the purpose, only what relates to the purpose of creation, connection to the Creator. In the connection to the Creator, you have freedom. In all other things, no."

### The Dial: Spin Again Until You Choose

He described a person as facing 360 possible directions, only one of which is toward the Creator; choosing any of the other 359 isn't really a choice at all, since a person is simply cycled back to the same offer:

> "You're like a dial, the hand of the clock. You're being offered this again. Do you want to go in that direction? Yes or no? Then spin around again, and again."

### What Actually Builds the Vessel

The states themselves — the "blows" and the "lollipops" — don't build a person's vessel; only the intention behind them does:

> "The blows and the lollipops, they don't build the vessel of the person. It's reinforcement towards the goal each time. That is the vessel. That is your intention."

## Part 5 — Dissemination: An Unsolved, Urgent Problem

### Books Alone Are Not Enough

Rav Laitman acknowledged that inner reinforcement through books and teachers helps only those who already have an inner lack, and admitted he does not have a full answer for reaching the wider population:

> "We need to find how to explain the general population that this is worthwhile. If a person has no inner urge, an inner lack, we need to awaken that urge. And I don't know how... the Baal HaSulam writes that it's a mystery how to do it."

### Reading Current Events as an Internal Problem

Asked about the state of the world, he described a deliberately different relationship to the news than most people have — not detachment, but reframing where the actual problem lies:

> "Our approach to what is happening is that it's time for us, that we have to solve the problem in the way that we believe is the solution... I regret that it's happening to the public, and they still don't lead to the recognition of evil. Where is the evil? It's not in the terrorism, in the terror act. It's in our internal state that is causing that terror act."

### Torah as the Opposite of the Ordinary View

Responding to a synagogue acquaintance's concern that he was "moving away from the Torah," Rav Laitman reframed the whole orientation:

> "I was always opposite from the Torah, because it's written the view of Torah is opposite from the view of the landlords [Baalei Batim, those focused on religion's externals]... For me, the Torah is something else. It's how to become better and lead to correction, both internal and external."

## Part 6 — Working for the Public, and No Looking Back

### The Pyramid Question

A participant asked why, if the world is structured like a pyramid with the group "at the top," there is any need to care about the general population at all. Rav Laitman's answer kept the hierarchy but reframed its purpose:

> "We are at the top. We have to make a connection with the Creator, and the rest of them stay the way they are, and according to the deficiencies that the Creator gave them, like He gave it to us, they'll join us in yearning, gradually reach from the bottom to the top and to Him."

### Restriction, Zero, and the Stony Hearts

Using the language of restriction, he described working for others as the actual mechanism by which a person's own vessel receives light:

> "If I make a restriction on myself, this is called 'more than necessity,' I don't need — and necessity is called zero. And I work... for the sake of the world, meaning I connect with the rest of the souls. When I annul myself before them, to me they become the light. In them I receive the light."

He connected this directly to "love your friend as yourself": "Without thinking about the public and serving the rule of Torah, which is love your friend as yourself, I cannot reach adhesion... It's a means to connect to the rest of the souls in order to be in adhesion with the Creator."

| Question raised | Rav Laitman's answer |
|---|---|
| Isn't inner work (study, restriction) separate from outer work (dissemination)? | They only look separate; without bestowing on the public, a person "cannot grow" — the two are one movement. |
| Why does the collective matter if we're at the "top" of the pyramid? | The rest advance from the deficiencies the Creator gave them, "gradually reach from the bottom to the top." |
| Do we need to literally provide for others materially? | No — "I'm talking about the internal correction," not material support. |

### No Calculations of the Past

Closing a run of questions about why a particular state or person arrives at a particular moment, Rav Laitman refused to let the discussion look backward at all:

> "I have to think from this moment onwards, where my eyes are looking... Why do I care about what happened a minute ago? It doesn't add anything to me. It only confuses me. At this minute, I receive the situation — that situation is from above, not a result of the previous moment."

### Closing Reflections

Participants closing out the lesson echoed this same point back in their own words: "Whatever happened in the past is just the Creator. We just have to associate it with Him and keep on moving on," and extended it to how they relate to each other: "We really never know who's sitting beside us... someone might be more advanced than us... we must never be harsh in judgment."

## Key Themes & Takeaways

1. Exile and revelation describe a person's own desires, not geography or ancestry — "Israel" and "the nations of the world" name where a person's 620 desires are currently aimed.

2. The only thing genuinely asked of a person is intention (kavanah) — determining the direction of yearning. Correcting, filling, and emptying the vessel is the Light's work, not the person's.

3. States before the barrier are darkness and confusion by design, not failure; the discernments gathered there are needed later, once a person begins working with faith above reason.

4. A person assesses his own state only by how much is revealed to him at that moment — never by comparison to an external standard — and even hard states are to be met with gratitude, since none are redundant to the goal.

5. Free choice exists in exactly one place: whether to move toward connection with the Creator. Every other apparent choice simply cycles a person back to the same offer; only the pace and direction of advancement are genuinely a person's own.

6. Dissemination to those without an inner lack is described as urgent and largely unsolved — books and inner reinforcement alone are not enough, and even world events are read as symptoms of an uncorrected internal state rather than separate problems.

7. Working for the collective — annulling oneself before the needs of others — is presented as the actual mechanism of a person's own adhesion, not a separate, optional project from inner work.

8. Advancement is only ever forward-looking; dwelling on why a particular state or person arrived is treated as a distraction, and judging others is discouraged since no one can see another's actual spiritual state.

## Key Terms & Concepts

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Olamot (Worlds) | Degrees of revelation or concealment of the Creator toward a person, from the root Ha'alama ("concealment/disappearance") — not physical realms. |
| Equivalence of Form | The alignment of a person's desires with the Creator's; named here as the actual meaning of "Israel" and of exiting exile. |
| Kavanah (Intention) | The direction of a person's yearning — the one thing a person determines, everything else being the Light's work. |
| Machsom (Barrier) | The point separating "before" states (darkness, confusion, gathering discernments) from "after" states (working with revealed faith). |
| Arvut / Love Your Friend as Yourself | The rule by which working for the rest of the souls becomes the mechanism of a person's own adhesion to the Creator. |
| Baalei Batim ("landlords") | Ordinary religious laypeople focused on Torah's externals — the view Rav Laitman describes his own approach as the opposite of. |
| Shlavei HaSulam ("Rungs of the Ladder") | The published collection of Rabash's articles; Vol. 2 contains the article read at the start of this lesson. |
| Yashar-El | Wordplay tracing "Israel" to "straight to the Creator" — the state of matching the Creator's equivalence of form. |
| Sha'ar HaGilgulim ("Gate of Reincarnations") | Chaim Vital's work on the Ari's teachings on reincarnation, referenced in the Q&A on souls and incarnation. |
| 620 Desires | The full set of a person's desires; corrected one by one so as to bestow, "turning" each from a Gentile to a Jew. |
