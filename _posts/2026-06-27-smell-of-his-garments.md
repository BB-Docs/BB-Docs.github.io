### The Opening Verse

Rabbi Zira comments on the verse (Genesis 27):

> *"And he smelled the smell of his garments and blessed them and said: the smell of my son is as the smell of a field that the Lord has blessed."*

Rabbi Zira teaches: do not read it as **b'gadav** (his garments) — read it as **b'ogdav** (his traitors). Even the traitors among them have a smell.

**The central question:** What is the connection between garments and traitors? How can betrayal carry a holy fragrance?

---

## Part 1 — The Nature of the Work and the Evil Inclination

### Two Levels of Work

The work divides into two tracks:

- **Work of the general public (Lo Lishma)** — working for reward, out of fear. Accessible to everyone: children, women, and those new to the path.
- **Work of the individual (Lishma)** — working purely to bestow, for the sake of the Creator. This is the higher path, revealed gradually.

As Maimonides explains: people are first taught to work out of fear and for reward. Only little by little, as they gain knowledge and wisdom, is the deeper secret revealed.

### The Evil Inclination — The Will to Receive

When a person decides to walk the path of Lishma — to do everything for the sake of the Creator — the evil inclination (the will to receive for oneself) immediately objects. A hard battle begins:

- The body asks: *"Why observe Torah and Mitzvot? What will you get out of it?"*
- The answer: The Creator said, *"I have created the evil inclination. I have created the Torah as a spice."* Through engaging in Torah, a person gains the strength to overcome it.
- The goal: To remove the governance of the *"old and foolish king"* (evil inclination) and take upon oneself the governance of the King of all kings — the Creator.

### How the Evil Grows With the Person

The evil inclination does not fight at full force immediately. It scales its resistance to the person's level of effort:

- When a person's efforts are small, the evil's resistance is small — there must always be a choice.
- As the person grows in Lishma work, the evil appears with greater force each time.
- This is why it is said: **"He who is greater than his friend — his inclination is greater than him."**

The evil does not give more bad deeds — it gives greater **pleasure in self-benefit**, making it increasingly hard to abandon self-interest and work to bestow.

**Key principle:** The balance is always maintained so that a person has a genuine choice — half guilty, half innocent. If one side dominated completely, there would be no free will.

## Part 2 — Descents, Betrayals, and Dissents

### What Is a Descent (Yeridah)?

When the evil inclination appears with greater force, a person enters a state of descent. During the descent:

- Every argument of the will to receive seems correct and justified.
- The person doubts the path: *"I have labored and toiled in vain — it is impossible to emerge from the governance of evil."*
- He betrays the work of the Creator — this is the "traitor" of the verse.
- After the Creator sends an awakening from Above, the person ascends again — only for the cycle to repeat.

### The Spies — A Parable for Descent

The complaint of the spies who slandered the Land of Israel is a parable for this inner state. During a descent the spies — who represent the person's inner voice of doubt — said:

> *"To this day we have not seen good in the world. We have toil in Torah and the house is empty. Who will be awarded that world?"*

During the descent a person does not lie — he says exactly what he feels. He cannot see the good. The error of the spies was that they failed to believe:

1. The revelation of evil comes from Above — it is given, not accidental.
2. The Creator Himself would give them a second nature: the desire to bestow.
3. Through the vessels of bestowal, the tzimtzum is lifted and *"the land flowing with milk and honey"* is revealed.

**The lesson:** Descents are required in order to fully reveal the evil, so that afterward the person knows with certainty that only the Creator can help. Help comes specifically on a **complete lack** — without a complete lack, there can be no complete help.

**Why gradually and not all at once?** If all the evil were revealed at once, a person could not begin the work at all — the bad would overwhelm and leave no room for choice. Therefore the evil is revealed proportionally to the good a person accumulates.

### "One Nation Shall Be Stronger Than the Other" — Rashi

Rashi interprets the verse about Jacob and Esau in the womb (Genesis 25): *"Two nations are in your womb... one nation shall be stronger than the other. When one rises, the other falls."*

In the Work, this describes the two forces within every person:

- **The old and foolish king** — the evil inclination / will to receive.
- **The King of all kings** — the good inclination / desire to bestow.

They grow together through opposition:

- Each time the good rises (ascent) → the evil falls, but then receives a greater addition of force for the next descent.
- Each time the evil rises (descent) → the good falls, but gains greater appreciation of the light upon its return.
- Both grow through the other. The righteous person has many ascents and many descents — each one deeper than the last.

**For one who does not walk the path of Lishma:** There are no ascents in between, so the evil has no reason to increase its force. They feel neither the depth of the evil nor the greatness of the good. The evil does not ruin them — but neither do they grow.

## Part 3 — The Advantage of Light From Within Darkness

### What a Person Must Learn During the Ascent

When in a state of ascent, a person naturally avoids thinking about his descents — it pains him. But Rabash teaches that remembering the descent during the ascent brings two essential benefits:

1. He learns how to guard himself better and minimize unnecessary falls.
2. He understands **"the advantage of the light from within the darkness"** — the contrast makes the light of the ascent shine far brighter and more meaningful.

By thinking about the betrayals experienced during the descents, all the descents are sweetened and filled with the blessing of the Creator — because he can now see that they were causing his ascent all along.

### Comparing the Two States

**During the ascent:** The person feels it is worthwhile to be a servant of the Creator. He feels the greatness and importance of the King. He wants to annul himself before the Creator like a candle before a torch.

**During the descent:** The body asks, *"What will you get out of canceling yourself from the entire world and caring only about bringing contentment to the Creator?"* Everything seems empty and pointless.

When a person considers both extremes together, the value of the ascent rises higher than he could have felt without the contrast. The descent itself elevates the ascent.

**Conclusion:** When a person completes the work, all the descents are corrected into merits. All the betrayals become fragrant — just as Jacob's garments (b'ogdav / traitors) carried the smell of the blessed field.

## Part 4 — Returning to the Verse: The Smell of His Traitors

### Why Jacob's Traitors Smelled Holy

Jacob achieved wholeness (shlemut) — not despite his descents and betrayals, but **through** them. Because all the bad within him had been fully revealed through his many ascents and descents, the help from Above could come completely. The descents — the betrayals — were the very vehicle through which Jacob was corrected.

This is why Rabbi Zira says: do not read "garments" — read "traitors." The smell that Isaac sensed was not only the fragrance of Jacob's Mitzvot and ascents. It was also the fragrance of his traitors — his falls and betrayals — which had been fully sweetened and corrected into merits.

> *"The smell of my son is as the smell of the field that the Lord has blessed" — this means he has already received all the bestowals of delight and pleasure from the Creator.*

### "Thus Far Your Kindness Has Assisted Us"

The article concludes with a prayer: *"Thus far your kindness has assisted us, and your mercies have not left us, the Lord our God."*

This prayer is spoken from a new state of understanding. The person now realizes:

- What felt as bad during the descents was also coming from the side of **kindness (Hesed)**.
- Even when providence felt like judgment, it was always mercy.
- *"Your mercies have not left us"* — even in the darkest descent, the Creator's mercy was present and operating.

---

## Key Themes & Takeaways

1. **Betrayals are not failures** — they are the mechanism of growth. Without descents, there are no ascents.
2. **The evil inclination grows with the person proportionally** — this is a sign of advancement, not weakness.
3. **Descents must be complete** for the help from Above to be complete. *"There is no light without a vessel."*
4. **During an ascent, remember the descent** — not to suffer, but to appreciate the contrast and learn from it.
5. **Everything — including what felt like bad — was always operating from the side of kindness and mercy.**
6. **The goal is not to avoid descents** but to pass through them with faith that the Creator is sending them, and to ask Him to bring you closer each time.

| **Dissent / Betrayal** | The inner state of doubting or abandoning the work during a descent. A mechanism of growth, not a moral failure. |
| **The Spies (Meraglim)** | A parable for the inner voice of doubt during descent; those who slandered the Land of Israel (= Kingdom of Heaven). |
| **Land of Israel / Milk and Honey** | Spiritually: the state of Dvekut with the Creator, attained through vessels of bestowal. |
| **"He who is greater — his inclination is greater"** | The harder the battle, the more advanced the person. |
| **Half Guilty / Half Innocent** | The principle of balanced free will — always maintained so a person can choose. |
| **"No light without a vessel"** | Complete revelation of evil (emptiness) is the prerequisite for complete help from Above. |
| **Advantage of Light From Darkness** | The contrast between ascent and descent makes the ascent brighter and more fully appreciated. |
| **Shlemut (Wholeness)** | The completed state Jacob reached — all descents corrected into merits, all traitors sweetened. |
| **Dvekut (Adhesion)** | Attachment to the Creator; the ultimate goal of the work. |
