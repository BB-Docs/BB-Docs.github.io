### Opening

After the group's own reading of the article (covering joy in bestowal, the right and left lines, and practical guidance for gatherings of friends), the session moves to Rav Laitman's recorded lecture and a long, dense Q&A. The central question the lecture pursues: since a person's nature, desires, and even his sense of self all come entirely from the Creator, where could free choice possibly exist at all — and how does the answer explain why the "assembly of friends" is the one place a person can actually build it?

---

## Part 1 — Where Free Choice Actually Comes From

### Nothing in a Person Is Truly His Own

Rav Laitman opens the recorded lecture with a stark claim: everything within a person — his nature, habits, education, the way his environment shaped him — comes entirely from the Creator. There is no "empty space" that is genuinely a person's own, no vacant place from which he acts independently. The only being that ever had such an empty space was Malchut of Ein Sof herself, through restriction.

### Building an "Empty Space" Through an External Force

To become Adam (human) rather than simply an instrument of the Creator's nature, a person needs an external force capable of pushing back his own nature — his built-in will to receive — so it no longer automatically governs him. This force cannot be found within himself, since anything found within him would still trace back to the Creator's nature. It can only come from something genuinely external: **the environment, other broken pieces of the shattered soul.**

### Hatred and Separation as the Raw Material of Free Choice

Adam HaRishon shattered into countless pieces, and separation — even something as simple as disregard, "he's not me" — arose between them. Rav Laitman's key move: **the very force of separation between people can be inverted into the force of adhesion.** Overcoming the natural hatred between broken pieces of the soul is the one place a person can extract genuine free-choice force — because this labor does not come from the Creator's built-in nature, but from a person's own struggle against it.

> *"There is no possibility to gain this force... without using the hatred that exists between the broken souls."*

## Part 2 — How a Person Comes to Resemble the Creator

### Discovering Who Governs You

A student asks directly: when can a person resemble the Creator? Rav Laitman lays out the sequence: first, a person discovers that his will to receive is entirely under the Creator's control — not his own. Next, he must judge whether this nature, compared to the Creator's, is good or bad. Recognizing it as bad (self-interested) leads eventually to **restriction** — refusing to let the will to receive govern, effectively pushing the Creator's direct influence away in that specific space, the way Malchut of Ein Sof did.

### The Light of A"B-SA"G and Bina's Freedom

The freed space created by restriction is filled through the light of A"B-SA"G, which gives the will to receive the capacity to no longer be ruled by pure reception — acquiring the quality of Bina, described as "free land." From there, the will to receive can be inverted into receiving in order to bestow — the actual meaning of *"Adam,"* from *Adameh la'Elyon*, "I will liken myself to the upper one."

### Why the Left Line Cannot Be Discussed in the Group

Answering a question about why two people must never discuss the left line together, Rav Laitman is blunt: anything that lowers a person's advance is forbidden in the group. The group should provide only upliftment, joy, and confidence; displaying weakness — even silently — harms both the person and the group, since **"there's no greater ego than just sitting at home and eating up oneself."** A person with a genuine concern should speak of it constructively — as something to work on together, not as a display of despair.

## Part 3 — Still, Vegetative, Animate, Speaking: A Ladder of Independence

Answering a question about degrees of independence, Rav Laitman walks through the four levels (from Shamati, "Still, Vegetative, Animate, Speaking"):

| Level | Degree of Independence |
|---|---|
| **Still** | No independence at all — entirely fulfills the "host's" (Creator's) will, like an embryo. |
| **Vegetative** | A little independence — can act somewhat against the host's will (bestow rather than self-benefit), but entirely bound to the norms of its "society" (all plants of a kind behave alike; no individuality). |
| **Animate** | More independence — each individual has distinct qualities and can move independently, but still cannot feel outside itself; no genuine "sense of others." |
| **Speaking** | Full independence — can work against its own nature, is not bound to its contemporaries, and, crucially, can genuinely feel others as itself — the "sense of others" that allows real care, and access to past and future rather than only the present. |

The speaking level's advantage is precisely this capacity to exit oneself and feel another's experience as one's own — which allows a person to acquire vessels beyond his own, and, through that, to receive what others attain as well.

## Part 4 — Q&A: Discovering That You Don't Want to Bestow

### Why Studying Alone Never Reveals the Evil Inclination

A student asks how the recognition of evil (a stage of the work) can actually be helped by the group. Rav Laitman's answer: recognition only comes through labor, exactly as in scientific discovery — nothing is revealed without exertion. If a person studies Kabbalah alone, gains understanding, even teaches others, and feels increasingly accomplished, **he will never discover that his own self-interested nature is bad** — everything about that path confirms and rewards him. This, he warns, is precisely the danger Baal HaSulam calls "the potion of death" in the introduction to the Study of the Ten Sefirot: a false, comfortable sense of advancement.

**The only way to actually feel one's nature as an obstacle** is to try to invest in the friends specifically because it's advised, and then discover, in that concrete attempt, how strongly one resists it. This resistance — not theoretical acknowledgment, but a felt refusal — is the real recognition of evil.

### Bad and Good Are Properties of Connection, Not of a Person

A crucial clarification follows: **neither a person nor his friend is "bad."** What is bad is the inability to connect. Separation is the evil; connection — specifically toward the Creator, not toward some unrelated shared project — is the good. This is why the evil inclination can only be discovered in the concrete work of connecting with friends toward the goal, not in solitary study.

### Where the Shechina Actually Dwells

Rav Laitman returns to a central image: the point of connection between friends — the "welding point," the place a person's self-interest is rejected in favor of genuine bonding — is where the Shechina (divine presence) actually dwells. Not within either person individually, but in the connection itself, built from screen and reflected light. **"You could say, but it's just friends — what does that have to do with the Creator? Under the condition that the connection between us will truly be the springboard to revelation... that's where we reveal Him."**

## Part 5 — What Does "Above Reason" Actually Mean, Practically?

Asked to clarify above reason once more, Rav Laitman distinguishes the ideal spiritual meaning (receiving the force of Bina, acting with total disregard for personal consequence) from what is practically available at a person's current stage: **accepting the advice of someone greater, even when it isn't yet self-evidently beneficial, and making that advice one's own reason.** This still operates within a person's own reason — but it is "above" the reason he had a moment before, since he has agreed to defer to someone wiser. This agreement will repeatedly break down and need to be remade, each time at a slightly higher level of discernment.

## Key Themes & Takeaways

1. **Free choice cannot come from anything already inside a person** — everything within is the Creator's nature; genuine choice-force can only be extracted from the labor of overcoming separation between people.
2. **The hatred and separation between broken souls is not simply an obstacle — it is the raw material free choice is built from**, when consciously inverted into a force of adhesion.
3. **The four levels (still, vegetative, animate, speaking) form a ladder of increasing independence**, culminating in the capacity to genuinely feel another's experience as one's own.
4. **Studying or teaching Kabbalah alone will never reveal a person's true self-interested nature** — only the concrete, resisted effort to invest in friends does, because solitary progress is self-confirming and can become "the potion of death."
5. **"Bad" and "good" describe connection and separation, not persons.** The evil inclination is discovered only in the attempt to connect with others toward the Creator, nowhere else.
6. **The Shechina dwells in the connection between people, not within any individual** — the welding point of rejected self-interest is the actual site of spiritual work.
7. **"Above reason," at a person's current stage, means adopting the advice of someone wiser as one's own reason** — not a mystical leap, but a repeated, practical act of deference that must be remade each time it breaks down.

## Key Terms & Concepts

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Restriction (Tzimtzum)** | Refusing to let the will to receive govern a person's action, creating an "empty space" in which genuine choice becomes possible — modeled on Malchut of Ein Sof. |
| **Light of A"B-SA"G** | The light that gives the restricted will to receive the capacity (the quality of Bina) to no longer be ruled by pure self-interest. |
| **Adam (from Adameh la'Elyon)** | "I will liken myself to the upper one" — becoming like the Creator by inverting the will to receive into receiving in order to bestow. |
| **Still / Vegetative / Animate / Speaking** | Four ascending levels of independence from the Creator's direct governance, culminating in the "sense of others" unique to the speaking level. |
| **Recognition of Evil** | Discovering, through concrete resisted effort (not theory), that one's self-interested nature obstructs connection; only attainable through real engagement with the group. |
| **The Potion of Death** | Baal HaSulam's term (Introduction to the Study of the Ten Sefirot) for the false sense of advancement produced by solitary study that never confronts one's actual resistance to bestowal. |
| **Welding Point / Connection** | The site between two people, built of screen and reflected light, where the Shechina is said to dwell — the actual location of spiritual work, not within either individual. |
| **Above Reason (practical sense)** | Adopting the advice of someone wiser as one's own operating reason, even without full independent verification — a repeatedly renewed act of deference, not blind abandonment of thought. |
