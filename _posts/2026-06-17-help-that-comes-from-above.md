## 1. What Is Help From Above?

Help from Above is anything that advances a person toward the purpose of creation — even when it doesn't feel pleasant or comfortable.

**Two forms of help:**
- Comfortable & pleasant — recognizable as good
- Difficult & challenging — exercises, burdens, failures, questions

Both forms have the same goal: to promote the person. Help begins when a person is truly ready to overcome and accept it in a beneficial way.

---

## 2. The Spiritual Journey — Stage by Stage

### Stage 1: Starting From Zero

Every person begins as a spiritual zero. They are given a desire — mysterious in origin, unknown in purpose. This desire instinctively drives the person to search for a place where it can be fulfilled.

- The person does not know where this desire comes from
- They move from place to place, scrutinizing and searching
- Even these scrutinies and movements come from Above

### Stage 2: Receiving a Method — The Burden Begins

Once a person settles in a place and begins receiving a spiritual method (a framework/teaching), the help given takes the form of a burden: questions, difficulties, and inner conflicts.

**The choice presented:**
- Run away → returns to the same suffering as before
- Advance → but doesn't know how

To advance, the person must invest more in observing and study until they realize: **the way forward is by asking for help from the Creator.**

### Stage 3: The Cry to the Creator

> *"It's difficult for a person, then he cries out to the Creator and the Creator helps him."* — Rabash

A person typically goes through many cycles of burden, failure, and disappointment before truly turning to the Creator. The realization is: *"Only from there will my help come."*

- When help is asked for, the Creator is revealed
- The person gains spiritual powers
- They can justify both the **left side** (the difficulties) AND the **right side** (the Creator's response)

### Stage 4: The King's Highway — and the Next Fall

After receiving help, the person feels stable again — in control, understanding, standing on both feet. Then:

- Alien thoughts arrive — fears, anxieties, lack of confidence
- New difficulties, questions, and pressures come from Above
- It seems like a setback, like being pushed away from the goal
- Depression and despair set in

But over time — through persistent effort, study, and accumulated suffering — the person once again receives help from Above, rises from the fall, and sees that the questions actually brought progress.

### Stage 5: The Cycle Continues

> *"Torah comes from Zion"* — from **Yetziyat** (exit) + **Tzion**. Torah emerges from the exits and returns to Kedushah.

Every cycle of falling out of Kedushah (holiness) and returning to it is how a person is spiritually built. The Torah — and the person himself — is formed through these very exits and returns.

## 3. The Mechanics of Spiritual Pressure

### The Upper Light Presses Constantly

The help from Above is perpetual. The Upper Light — the "Father of Creation" — constantly presses on the soul of every person, with the intention to benefit His creations.

### The Soul's Two Forces

The soul is built of two opposite forces, which is why the same pressure feels different at different times:

| Force | Source |
|---|---|
| Will to Receive | Natural to the soul — its inherent nature |
| Will to Bestow | Comes from the shuddering of the vessels — the point in the heart |

### Two Lines, One Light

Because the soul includes these two forces, the same Upper Light produces two felt experiences:

- **Left Line** — the state of difficulty, questions, burden (seemingly bad)
- **Right Line** — the state of advancement, clarity, connection (seemingly good)

This is how a person grows — on both legs, alternating between the two lines.

### Two Angels — One Direction

The Creator sends only one kind of pressure: the Upper Light seeking to enter the vessel. But the vessel perceives it as two forces:

- The Good Angel / Good Inclination (Yetzer Tov)
- The Bad Angel / Evil Inclination (Yetzer HaRa)

In truth, both operate in the **same direction** — toward the person's spiritual advancement. This requires genuine **faith above reason.**

---

## 4. The Role of Study and Society

The study group and friends play a critical role: they must constantly remind a person that everything happening — to him, around him, in the world — comes only from the thought of creation, which is to benefit His creations.

- Every moment operates to bring the person closer to the goal
- In each state, the person must find the "correct manner" — how the Creator's influence is arriving
- Within that influence, the person discovers the bestowal

## 5. Levels of Concealment

| Level | Experience | Transition |
|---|---|---|
| **Double Concealment** | "I feel bad. That's it. I feel bad." No awareness of the Creator. | Begin to feel that this "bad" comes from the Creator |
| **Single Concealment** | Cannot see the Creator but feels His presence within the difficulty | Justify/discover the Creator repeatedly until He is revealed |
| **Revelation (Crossing the Barrier)** | The Creator is revealed. This is the whole point. | This is "crossing the barrier" (Machsom) |

## 6. Key Terms Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Kedushah** | Holiness / the spiritual domain the person is being elevated toward |
| **Rabash** | Rabbi Baruch Ashlag — author referenced in the lesson |
| **Left Line** | State of difficulty, burden, questions — produced by the same Upper Light |
| **Right Line** | State of clarity, advancement, connection — the other side of the same pressure |
| **Yetzer Tov** | The Good Inclination — the Good Angel |
| **Yetzer HaRa** | The Evil Inclination — the Bad Angel. Both ultimately serve the same purpose |
| **Double Concealment** | The Creator is hidden and suffering feels purposeless |
| **Single Concealment** | The Creator is hidden but the person senses His hand in what is happening |
| **Machsom** | The barrier — crossing it means the Creator is revealed |
| **Torah from Zion** | Torah emerges from Yetziyah (exit from Kedushah) and the return — built through the cycle |
| **Point in the Heart** | The seed of the will to bestow within the soul; the spiritual spark |
| **Faith Above Reason** | Trusting the process even when evidence suggests otherwise — essential to advancement |

---

## 7. Core Principles — Summary

- All states — good and difficult alike — are sent from Above with one purpose: to benefit and advance the person
- The Creator sends one pressure (Upper Light); the soul perceives it as two — the Good and Bad Inclinations
- Spiritual growth happens on both legs: alternating between the Left Line and Right Line
- The key moment is the cry to the Creator — turning to Him for help in the difficulty
- Every fall contains within it the seed of the next rise; every exit from Kedushah precedes a deeper return
- In each and every state, the task is to discover that this state is held from Above
