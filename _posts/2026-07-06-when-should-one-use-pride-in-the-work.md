### Opening

After the group reads a portion of the Rabash article together, Rav Laitman turns to a separately recorded lesson from January 25, 2002, built around a practical question: if a person only gains any real control over the soul's inner forces through effort exerted during states of descent, what is actually left for him to do — and where is genuine free choice, if the Creator seems to determine everything from Above anyway? The lecture and its long Q&A trace this question through the stages of the soul's development, the story of Abraham read as an inner drama, and the difference between ordinary intuition and the soul's own "sixth sense."

---

## Part 1 — Governing the Soul Happens Specifically in Descent

### Effort Only Counts When Exerted During Descent

Rav Laitman explains that to "control the soul" means to rule over the forces, qualities, and urges within it — the vessel by which a person feels the spiritual world. This governing is specifically achieved through exertion made **during states of descent**, when a person is deliberately given the sense of being distant from spirituality, uninterested, finding nothing attractive in it.

> Despite not feeling any interest, he understands that this feeling was given to him deliberately, and so despite not feeling any interest, he still goes for it — holds it by the teeth, as we say, acquiring spirituality.

**The key distinction:** states of ascent, by contrast, are given as an "awakening from above" — they come as a gift from the Creator and are not credited to the person's own account. Only what a person does during descent, by his own exertion, is counted as genuine advancement.

## Part 2 — Abraham's Inner Theater: Why Ask at All?

### The Question

A student raises the puzzle carried over from the article: the Creator directly instructed Abraham to circumcise himself, so why did Abraham need to go ask his friends whether to obey?

### The Answer: Development Through Incarnations

Rav Laitman reframes the whole story as an account of the soul's development. Humanity develops over many incarnations at a beastly level, chasing sex, honor, money, and knowledge — the **corporeal stage** of the soul's growth. Eventually, after enough such development, a lack is revealed for something beyond these pleasures: **the point in the heart**. This new urge toward something "beyond the world" is what the story means by "the Creator" prompting Abraham to take on a new, higher state.

**The characters are inner qualities, not separate people:**

- Mamre, Sarah, Abraham's household, the field, and even the surrounding Bedouins are described as **actors in an inner theater** — each representing a quality within the person that is either for or against the new state being taken on.

### Why the Opposition Is Necessary

Rav Laitman explains that if a person simply obeyed instructions outright, without any partaking of his own, he would remain on the level of "still, vegetative, animate" nature — bonded to the Creator's will completely, but with no distinct achievement of his own. This would defeat **the purpose of creation**, which is to bring the created being to resemble the Creator through the being's own recognition and effort, not simply to move him like a puppet.

## Part 3 — Levels of Nature and the One Real Choice

### Still, Vegetative, Animate, and Speaking

Rav Laitman lays out the four levels of nature: the still, vegetative, and animate levels do only what nature (the Creator) determines, with no choice at all — "what nature determines, the Creator determines." Only the **speaking level (man)** has room to criticize, disagree, even rebel — and then, from that opposition, to choose to become like the Creator.

### Kabbalah's Education vs. Religious Education

> Our education, the Kabbalistic education, is very opposite to education on the street. Religion simply educates a person to be like a machine — get up and do this, do that. Kabbalah is the opposite: it develops in a person's senses the capacity to draw out criticism.

Kabbalistic study deliberately draws criticism and even rebellion out of a person — disagreement with, and even a wish to reject, the Creator — so that a person comes to understand where these opposite qualities come from, compares them with the Creator's own qualities, and from that comparison chooses to resemble Him. This is what builds "Adam" out of a person: **Adam comes from the word "Edameh L'Elyon" — to resemble the One above.**

## Part 4 — Intuition vs. the Sixth Sense

### Where Does Resistance to Advancement Come From?

A student asks: if there is such a strong inner intuition guiding a person, why is there still resistance to advancing?

### Two Different Things

Rav Laitman distinguishes two senses that are often confused:

| | Basis | Can It Reach Spirituality? |
|---|---|---|
| **Intuition** | Rooted entirely in beastly experience and the ordinary mind; knows nothing beyond what it has already seen | No — spirituality runs on completely different laws than the beastly mind can access |
| **The Sixth Sense** | Exists, in potential, in every person; is the sense proper to the soul itself | Yes — once a person feels a lack for it, a specific method (Kabbalah) exists to develop it |

**The takeaway:** a person cannot rely on intuition, however strong it feels, to find the spiritual path — only the sixth sense, deliberately developed, can perceive it.

## Part 5 — The Desert, the Book, and the Narrow Trail

### Freedom of Choice Must Be Taught, Not Just Declared

Rav Laitman uses the image of a person standing in a desert where every direction looks identical — "three hundred and sixty degrees, it's all the same." A person cannot simply be told "you are free to choose"; he has to be shown *where* the freedom actually is, since there is one specific, narrow path meant for him personally, invisible amid what looks like a directionless expanse.

> It's a path, long but very narrow, and it's in a desert where you don't see the path... if you don't do it, then another incarnation, and another. Time passes like that, because we are below the plane of spiritual life — it doesn't bother anyone, it's a useless life, there's no calculation regarding it.

### How the Method Works in Practice

Without receiving something from outside — from the wisdom itself — a person cannot advance, because he is not built to see the choice directly. Study draws down the **surrounding light (Ohr Makif)**, which works on the point in the heart and produces an attraction toward the source of that light; this attraction is itself what fixes the direction of choice. Preparation matters: sitting with the correct book, having first consolidated exactly what one wants to achieve and through what.

## Part 6 — Descent, Despair, and Awakening from Below

### Where Does the Strength for Self-Effort Come From?

Asked where a person finds the power to make his own effort ("awakening from below") during a state of descent, Rav Laitman explains that a person is deliberately left without support from Above during descent — otherwise the movement would not be his own.

**The double bind of descent:** a person in descent has neither the present (no felt state) nor the future (no felt promise from afar) to rely on — and it is exactly this combination that produces despair.

**Why this is intentional:** it shows the person what his true state is, without any correction from Above, and lets him see by contrast how much better it is to draw on the forces that come from Above. These difficult states themselves teach and build the person — unlike simply being handed fulfillment, which would not be genuinely his own.

## Part 7 — Closing Workshop: What the Group Took From the Lesson

In the brief closing round, participants and Rav Laitman restated the session's core points together:

- **Lack of taste and lowliness in the work is exactly where exertion counts** — this is the state that is "counted" toward a person's own account.
- **All the Kabbalists' stories play out as an inner theater**, with figures like Abraham and Sarah each representing a quality or role within the person.
- **There is nothing to determine except one thing** — advancing in the direction of resembling the Creator.
- **The forces of the soul, in all their contradiction, exist so free choice can be exercised** through them.
- **Light develops the soul**, and a person's actions in this world should aim to draw down as much of that light as possible.
- **The Creator arranges states of remoteness deliberately**, so a person will come to recognize, on his own, what has already been placed within him.
- Abraham is named as "a champion" of these difficult states — the group touches briefly (in a garbled aside evoking an averted sacrifice) on how demanding, and yet formative, these states were for him.
- **Despair during descent, and the eventual correction that follows it, is what allows continued advancement** — the deficiency felt during descent is what the later correction fills.
- The distinction between **intuition and the sixth sense** was restated as a closing point.

## Key Themes & Takeaways

1. **Control over the soul is gained specifically through effort exerted during descent** — states of ascent are gifts from Above and are not credited as a person's own achievement.
2. **The story's characters (Mamre, Sarah, the household, the field) are qualities within a person**, not external figures — the whole narrative unfolds as an inner theater.
3. **Opposition and inner resistance exist so that free choice is possible at all** — without them, a person would simply be moved like a puppet, defeating the purpose of creation.
4. **Only the "speaking" level of nature has real choice** — the still, vegetative, and animate levels are fully bonded to the Creator's will with no distinct achievement of their own.
5. **Ordinary intuition cannot find the spiritual path** — only the soul's own sixth sense, developed through study, can perceive it, since spirituality runs on entirely different laws than the beastly mind.
6. **Freedom of choice has to be taught, not just declared** — like being shown that a single narrow path exists in what looks like a directionless desert.
7. **Descent is intentionally left unsupported by present feeling or future promise** — the resulting despair is what teaches and builds a person, and prepares him to value the forces that come from Above.
