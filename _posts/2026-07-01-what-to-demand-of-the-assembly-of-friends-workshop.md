### Opening

This session is structured as a live workshop: short readings from Rabash's article are paired with clips of Rav Laitman and a moderated Q&A, with questions arriving from students in Ukraine, Turkey, and elsewhere. The organizing question, asked directly at the outset: what, concretely, do we ask of a gathering of friends — and where, exactly, is the Creator found when people make the effort to connect?

---

## Part 1 — Why Separation Is the Raw Material of Freedom

The session replays Rav Laitman's core teaching on free choice: a person needs an external force to stop being simply operated by the Creator's nature — and that force cannot be found within himself, since anything found there would still just be the Creator's nature again. It can only come from the environment: **specifically, from using the very separation and hatred between broken souls as a springboard**, extracting from that separation its opposite — the force of adhesion.

> *"I have no other option to equip by force to such an action without using a hatred between the broken souls."*

**Why the gathering exists at all:** if a person never truly invests in the friends, his own bad nature is never revealed — "I will never reveal it... we're studying, everything is okay." Without revelation of the bad, there is no genuine turn to the Creator. The whole purpose of Kabbalah, in this framing, is a device for revealing what is bad in a person — and the assembly of friends is where that revelation actually happens.

## Part 2 — Practical Q&A: How to Handle Problems in the Group

### Can We Discuss Problems in the Ten?

A student from Ukraine asks directly: if the left line (dwelling on problems) is forbidden in the group, how should real problems in the ten actually be discussed? Rav Laitman's answer: **connection must come first.** Diving straight into a problem only entrenches it further. The group must first reach a genuine state of inner connection — building it through mutual appreciation and praise of the society — and only from that elevated place should a specific obstacle be addressed, always framed as "how do we rise above this together," never as dwelling in the problem itself.

> *"If we reach that state of true connection, believe me, all of a sudden these problems will dissipate."*

### Should We Discuss a Friend's Material (Corporeal) Circumstances?

Another student asks whether daily meetings should address a friend's external, corporeal state, or only spiritual matters — and what "my friend's corporeality is my spirituality" actually means. The answer: the group does not turn into a support group for material troubles (debts, health as such) as an end in itself. Rather, the group cares about a friend's basic conditions — family, income, shelter — specifically so that friend is free to direct his full attention to spiritual work. When friends gather to pray for someone's health or hardship, **the prayer itself is the actual spiritual work** — a way of using the friend's corporeal vessel as an opening for spiritual connection, not merely acting as a social-aid committee.

### When Is a Meal a Spiritual Occasion?

Rav Laitman is quoted affirming that a shared meal can function on the same level as a lesson — "not to eat, but to work" — provided the same underlying purpose (connection, revealing the Creator through the bond between friends) frames the gathering.

## Part 3 — Judging a Friend to the Side of Merit, in Practice

### What to Do When a Friend's Behavior Seems Wrong

Asked what it actually means to judge a friend favorably when his behavior genuinely looks wrong, Rav Laitman describes a practical pause: instead of immediately confronting the friend, ask what the situation is actually telling *you* — "who is talking to me through him?" This reframing (closer to above reason than ordinary judgment) often reveals, on reflection, what a genuinely valuable friend and burning point in the heart the person actually has.

### Two Paths: Nimrod's Way or Abraham's Way

Rav Laitman contrasts two responses to friction between people: **Nimrod's path** — mutual hatred, everyone going their own way — versus **Abraham's path** — rising above the friction to build connection (the Temple, not the Tower of Babel).

### The Real Revelation Is About the Connection, Not the Friend

Returning to the session's central point: the friend himself is not "bad." What gets revealed through investing effort in a friend is a person's *own* resistance to connection — discovering, concretely, how much he does not actually want it. **"It's either separation or connection, and that's where you need to work."** This is why solitary effort never reveals this resistance — only real, invested contact with another person does.

## Part 4 — Building the Vessel: The Point of Connection

### Where the Divine Presence Actually Resides

Rav Laitman restates the session's central image: if there is genuine work on the connection between friends, the "point of fusion" between them is where the divine presence actually resides — not within any individual person. This connection is described as a screen and reflected light — the vessel itself, formed by rejecting self-interest.

> *"Everything that happens, corporeality, spirituality, between us, the friends — all that we entertain is the revelation of the Creator, and that's where we need to work."*

### A Practical Sequence for the Gathering

A student raises an apparent contradiction: Rabash says not to bring dissatisfaction to the gathering, yet Rav Laitman also encourages openly sharing what's in one's heart. The resolution offered: the sequence matters. First, however long it takes — an hour, several hours, even a full day, in the speaker's words — the group must build genuine connection through mutual appreciation and praise. **Only from that elevated place** should any specific problem be addressed; skipping straight to the problem after a token few minutes of appreciation defeats the purpose. Problems, in this account, were given specifically so the group would be driven to build real connection in the first place — once that connection is reached, "the problems dissipate," not because they're ignored, but because they're now addressed from a genuinely different vantage point.

## Part 5 — Practical Exercises and What Can Actually Be Given

### Praising a Friend Publicly

Asked whether it's appropriate to publicly praise a specific friend (e.g., the head of the ten) for a contribution, Rav Laitman gives a simple test: **if it contributes to the group's connection, do it; if it doesn't, don't.**

### What a Friend Can Actually Give You

Asked what "buying" a friend actually means in practice, the answer is that what one friend gives another is love, and the shared importance of the goal — not material assistance, entertainment, or shared external interests. This exchange is described as being tested precisely through effort: attempting good actions toward friends and noticing real resistance is what reveals a person's nature as an obstacle — described again as the mechanism by which "the evil inclination" becomes visible, not through theory but through invested practice.

## Key Themes & Takeaways

1. **Separation between people is not just an obstacle — it is the raw material free choice is built from**, when consciously used as a springboard toward connection.
2. **Real problems in the group should never be addressed first.** Genuine connection has to be built before any specific difficulty is discussed, or the group will only entrench the problem.
3. **A friend's corporeal circumstances matter insofar as they free him for spiritual work** — the group's care is not material aid for its own sake, but prayer and support that opens a path to genuine spiritual connection.
4. **What gets revealed through investing in friends is one's own resistance, not the friend's faults.** Judging to the side of merit is a discipline against the impulse to confront rather than reflect.
5. **The divine presence is found in the "point of fusion" between people** — the connection itself, not any individual — built from rejecting self-interest.
6. **Every practice (public praise, discussing a friend's circumstances, sharing a personal difficulty) is tested by one criterion: does it contribute to genuine connection?**
7. **What one friend can actually give another is love and shared importance of the goal** — not material help, shared external interests, or entertainment.

## Key Terms & Concepts

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Force of Arvut** | The mutual guarantee/responsibility that frees a friend to focus purely on spirituality, built through the group's collective support. |
| **Judging to the Side of Merit** | Pausing before confronting a friend's apparently wrong behavior, and asking what the situation reveals about oneself instead. |
| **Nimrod's Path vs. Abraham's Path** | Mutual hatred and separation vs. rising above friction to build genuine connection — the Tower of Babel vs. the Temple. |
| **Point of Fusion / Welding Point** | The site of connection between friends where the divine presence is said to actually reside, built of screen and reflected light. |
| **"My Friend's Corporeality Is My Spirituality"** | The principle that caring for a friend's material circumstances (through prayer and support, not material handouts as an end) is itself genuine spiritual work. |
| **Right Line / Left Line (in Group Settings)** | Right line: mutual appreciation and connection, the required starting point for any gathering. Left line: dwelling on problems, permitted only after real connection is built, and only briefly. |
| **The Test for Any Practice** | Whether an action (public praise, discussing a friend's state, sharing dissatisfaction) contributes to the group's genuine connection — the deciding question in every case raised in this session. |
