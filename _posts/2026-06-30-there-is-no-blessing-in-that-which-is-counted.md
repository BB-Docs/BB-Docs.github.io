The new article asks a related but broader question: if the Creator's whole purpose is to reveal Himself and do good, why does He conceal Himself from a person at all — and, practically, what is a person actually supposed to do with the body, its pleasures, and its desires while working to remove that concealment? ## Part 1 — Completing "There Is No Blessing in That Which Is Counted"

### The Order of the Two Blessings

Where the shorter recording cut off, the lesson goes on to lay out the full order of the Work as three steps:

1. **A blessing before** — the right line, **Chesed**: a person bestows without wanting anything in return.
2. **The gift itself** — the left line, **Chochmah**: a person is rewarded with "the secrets of Torah," received specifically in the vessels of reception, which is why it is called a count — "he looks at what he received so as to know he should thank Him." This is also where the risk of death (separation) appears, "such as there was in the death of the seven kings in the world of Nekudim."
3. **A blessing after** — drawing **Chassidim** again, so the reception that just happened doesn't pull the aim back toward self-interest.

**This is why Israel were blessed "in the beginning and in the end,"** and why no death occurred among them — the count was held safely between two blessings.

### Three Questions, Answered

The lesson summarizes its own resolution as a short list of questions and answers:

1. Did the Creator need Israel to report their number so He could know it? No — the counting was so that *Israel* would know and attain the light of Chochmah, "the light of seeing."
2. Why is there death where there is no blessing? Because the light that is counted is the light of Chochmah, received in vessels of reception — and the act of receiving can override the intention to bestow.
3. Why is a blessing needed both before and after? Because the order of the Work begins with the aim to reach **Lishmah**, expressed first as a blessing of pure bestowal (Chesed), then continues through the receiving of Chochmah (left line), then needs a second blessing of Chassidim (right line) to hold the result safely in place — together forming the **middle line**.

> *"Chochmah shines in the vessels of reception, which is called left. They need correction so that one is not drawn after the act of reception. In that state, the light of Chassidim, which works with the vessels of bestowal, must be drawn."*

## Part 2 — A Second Article, Same Title: Why the Creator Conceals Himself

### Concealment So the Creature Can Work Alone

The lesson turns to the second article. The Creator and the created being exist together, but for the created being to become truly independent — to reach the Creator's degree *by himself* — the Creator must hide, leaving the created being room to do the work alone. **If the created being does not perform the process through his own labor, he will never acquire the vessels capable of feeling wholeness and eternity** — the actual goal.

This is why a person must descend, "from the root of his soul," all the way down to this lowest world, where everything is concealed — even though, in truth, he already exists in full connection with the Creator at the end of correction. **That state is simply hidden from him.**

### The Bank Account We Can't Feel

Rabash's image: it is as though someone deposited a great sum of money into your account, but you were never told — so you go on feeling poor. **This is how we feel now, because we do not perceive our actual state.** It is as if a person is missing a sense, like sight or hearing — so he cannot perceive the reality he is actually in.

### Mutual Concealment Builds the Degrees

As a person succeeds in making a screen on himself — hiding his will to receive from the light, so he is moved only by the purpose of creation rather than by raw pleasure — the Creator correspondingly lowers a screen from His own side and reveals more of Himself. The more screens a person builds, the more degrees he climbs.

This produces a changing relationship: sometimes the Creator is concealed, sometimes the person is concealed, sometimes both are hidden from each other, sometimes both are revealed. **These alternating states are what build a person's vessels**, until at the final degree he becomes, in the article's words, truly and completely like the Creator — "Return, O Israel, to the Lord your God."

## Part 3 — Building the Vessels: From Bestowal to Reception

### The Order: Chassidim First, Then Chochmah

A person's vessels are corrected in order. First, he acquires a screen only over the **light of Chassidim** — purely "in order to bestow." This is enough to *see* the Creator (to be, in the article's phrase, "delighted in mercy"), but seeing is not yet equivalence. To actually **be like** the Creator — equivalence of action, not just appreciation — a person must also learn to *receive* in order to bestow, putting the vessels of reception themselves to use rather than avoiding them.

### Where the Vessels of Reception Come From

The vessels of reception are not naturally in a person's possession — they develop over many incarnations, through:

- **Beastly pleasures** — food, rest, sex, home: pleasures a person would want even alone.
- **Human pleasures** — money, honor, knowledge: deficiencies and pleasures that exist only because other people are around.
- **The point in the heart** — a desire unrelated to this world at all, the beginning of the soul, which can only be enlarged by the light itself ("the light builds the vessel, corrects the vessel, fills the vessel — the light does everything").

### Israel and "the Nations of the World"

In this framework, **vessels of bestowal are called "Israel"** — they yearn only for connection with the Creator — and **vessels of reception are called "the nations of the world."** To advance, a person must take the broader will to receive (described here as joining the vessels associated with "the nations" into the vessels of "Israel") and bring it under the same corrected intention, repeating this at every higher degree.

### This World, the Next World, and the Barrier

Working to receive in order to receive — at any level — is called **"this world."** Working to bestow in order to bestow, or to receive in order to bestow, is called **"the next world,"** or spirituality. **The transition between the two is called the barrier (Machsom).** What determines which world a person is in is never the object itself, but the attitude behind it.

## Part 4 — Q&A: Why Kabbalah Doesn't Ask You to Renounce the World

A student asks why, instead of gradually correcting the will to receive, a person shouldn't simply withdraw from the world the way ascetics do — take only minimal food and water, restrict everything else, and slowly bring intention to whatever remains.

### Intention, Not Magnitude

Rav Laitman's answer: **Kabbalah does not relate to the size of a pleasure at all — only to the attitude behind it.** Whether someone fasted all day or sat in restaurants from morning to night says nothing by itself about their spiritual state; either could be done "in order to receive" or "in order to bestow." The wisdom of Kabbalah, properly speaking, doesn't even register bodily pleasures as pleasures — its actual subject begins only beyond the barrier, with spiritual pleasure, meaning the revelation of the Creator.

### A Superseded Practice

Older instruction — "eat bread with salt, drink a measured amount of water, sleep on the ground, engage in Torah" (Pirkei Avot) — described a real path of self-affliction that, the lesson says, worked in its time but **"for us, no longer works."** The sages of the Mishnah already taught the newer means: **"one should always practice Torah and Mitzvot, even Lo Lishmah, and from Lo Lishmah he will come to Lishmah"** — relying on the reforming light drawn through Torah and Mitzvot rather than on physical penance.

### Examples and a Warning

Great Kabbalists lived very differently from one another — some rich, some poor; the Ari himself was a merchant, and Rabash lived in real poverty in Jerusalem and later, by Rav Laitman's account, "wouldn't spare any pleasure." **None of it mattered**, because correction happens only through the light drawn by Torah and Mitzvot, never through what the body is or isn't given.

The lesson warns explicitly against false asceticism as a source of pride:

> *"I knew a guy in Bnei Brak... he used to walk with shoes without soles in order to suffer... it only brings pride. It doesn't correct."*

Judaism's external posture is described as the opposite of renunciation: marry, raise a family, eat, drink, don't abstain — because withholding from the body, when not genuinely required as a specific spiritual correction, accomplishes nothing and risks turning into self-congratulation.

## Part 5 — Q&A: Abraham, Egypt, and the Two Ways to Bestow

### Abraham's Search

A student's question about envy and lust leads into the Midrashic story of Abraham, who as a young man began questioning where the stars, the moon, and the world itself came from, until **the Creator was revealed to him.** Abraham then began sitting at the opening of his tent, drawing people in with hospitality and disseminating the idea that there is a Creator worth knowing; Sarah did the same among the women. The Creator told him He would make of him a great nation — specifically *through* bringing his descendants into Egypt, into a great difficulty, because **the troubles a person feels are what help him aim himself toward the goal.**

### Egypt as the Will to Receive

In the article's reading, **Egypt (Pharaoh) is the entire will to receive.** The point in the heart — called Abraham, or Israel, longing only for the Creator — needs to take on real substance, and that substance is the will to receive itself. A person must, in effect, go down into that will to receive, find it good at first, then suffer from it, and only once he no longer wants it for its own sake can he "steal" it — take it under the condition of restriction — and bring it back out to be corrected by the light of Torah received after the Exodus.

### Two Ways to "Bestow"

This sharpens the difference between two phrases used earlier in the lesson:

| | Bestowing in order to bestow | Receiving in order to bestow |
|---|---|---|
| What happens | A person restricts the vessels of reception and doesn't touch them — pure withholding | A person actually uses the vessels of reception, but with the intention to bestow |
| Status | A preparation — small vessels that come directly "from the Creator" | The real, full-scale work — the actual vessels a person must correct |
| Analogy in the lesson | The point of Abraham before Egypt | Israel's work with the vessels taken out of Egypt |

## Part 6 — Q&A: Envy, Lust, Honor — and a Glimpse Beyond Reward and Punishment

### Tools for Acceleration

Drawing on Baal HaSulam's introduction to *Panim Meirot U'Panim Masbirot*, the lesson identifies **envy, lust, and honor** as the three forces that move a person from one level of pleasure to the next — used correctly, they hasten development. The distinction is in their direction:

- **Constructive envy** — wanting another's good *because it is good for them*, which in turn pulls a person to want that good for himself too.
- **Destructive envy** — wanting the other *not* to have it, which produces no advancement at all.

The same three forces — envy, lust, and honor — exist, in a corrected form, in relation to the Creator as well ("the glory of heaven").

### Two Kinds of Failure, Two Higher Degrees

The lesson sketches a short ladder of inner states:

- **Double concealment** — acting badly, even doing seemingly good things, while still not wanting the surrounding light at all; done "with malice."
- **Regular concealment** — once the surrounding light illuminates a little and a person begins to sense there is "something, someone" there, the same kind of action becomes an unintentional error rather than a willful one.
- **Reward and punishment** — beyond the barrier, a person's intention is genuinely to bestow, but the relationship is still governed by fear — "fear at the expense of the Creator" — because the person's actions still seem to determine an outcome.
- **Eternal love** — a still higher degree where a person knows the Creator's love is unconditional and uncalculated; reward and punishment, as a framework, disappears entirely.

## Key Themes & Takeaways

1. **A blessing must surround a count on both sides.** The Work's structure is bestowal (Chesed) → reception of the gift (Chochmah, the count) → bestowal again (Chassidim) — together forming the middle line that keeps reception anchored to bestowal.
2. **Concealment is not a withholding of love — it's the condition for independence.** The Creator hides specifically so the created being can do the work of correction by his own labor and so actually earn the vessels of wholeness and eternity.
3. **A person's vessels develop in order: bestowal first, then reception with the aim to bestow.** Seeing the Creator ("delighted in mercy") is not the same as full equivalence of action, which requires putting the vessels of reception themselves to corrected use.
4. **Kabbalah evaluates intention, never the size or kind of pleasure.** Older ascetic practice ("bread, salt, water, ground") has been superseded by the reforming light drawn through Torah and Mitzvot, even when practiced Lo Lishmah.
5. **Self-imposed suffering for its own sake produces pride, not correction.** True Kabbalists have lived in poverty and in wealth alike; what corrects a person is never what is or isn't given to the body.
6. **Egypt represents the will to receive itself, which must be "stolen" — taken under restriction — before it can be corrected by the light.** This is the deeper meaning of Israel's descent into, and exodus from, Egypt.
7. **Envy, lust, and honor are tools, not just temptations.** Directed toward wanting another's good rather than resenting it, they accelerate a person's own development; further along, even the framework of reward and punishment gives way to a love beyond calculation.

## Key Terms & Concepts

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Middle Line** | The balance of the right line (Chesed/blessing) and left line (Chochmah/the count), achieved by drawing Chassidim again after reception so it stays anchored to bestowal. |
| **Lishmah / Lo Lishmah** | For Her sake / not yet for Her sake. A person begins Lo Lishmah and is reformed toward Lishmah through the light drawn by Torah and Mitzvot. |
| **Screen (Masach)** | The means by which a person hides his will to receive from raw pleasure so as to be moved only by the purpose of creation; building screens corresponds to climbing degrees. |
| **Degrees (Madregot)** | Stages of an alternating, mutual process of concealment and revelation between a person and the Creator, which gradually build a person's spiritual vessels. |
| **Delighted in Mercy (Chafetz Chesed)** | The state of wanting nothing for oneself, working purely in bestowal — sufficient to "see" the Creator, but not yet full equivalence of action. |
| **Vessels of Bestowal / "Israel"** | The aspect of a person, or of the corrected vessel, that yearns only for connection with the Creator. |
| **Vessels of Reception / "Nations of the World"** | The broader will to receive, which must eventually be joined to and corrected under the intention of the vessels of bestowal. |
| **This World / The Next World** | This world: working in order to receive (at any level). The next world: working to bestow, or to receive in order to bestow. |
| **The Barrier (Machsom)** | The transition point between working in order to receive and working in order to bestow. |
| **Egypt / Pharaoh** | In this article's reading, the entirety of the will to receive, which must be "stolen" — taken under restriction — before it can be corrected. |
| **Bestowing in Order to Bestow vs. Receiving in Order to Bestow** | Withholding from the vessels of reception entirely (a preparatory, small vessel) vs. actually using the vessels of reception with the intention to bestow (the real, full-scale work). |
| **Envy, Lust, and Honor** | Three drives that, directed well (wanting another's good rather than resenting it), accelerate a person's spiritual development; directed badly, they produce no advancement. |
| **Double Concealment / Regular Concealment** | Acting against an unfelt light, with malice (double concealment) vs. acting in error once some light is already sensed (regular concealment). |
| **Reward and Punishment vs. Eternal Love** | An intermediate stage where bestowal is still tied to fear/consequence, vs. a higher, barely graspable degree where the Creator's love is unconditional and reward and punishment no longer apply. |
