### Opening

The recorded lecture opens with a stark claim: a person is entirely a result of the will to receive operating in him — its size, and how corrected it currently is. The lecture and the long Q&A that follows push this claim to its most uncomfortable edge: if a person really is just a passive outcome of the light acting on his desire in each moment, where — if anywhere — is there room for a self, for scrutiny, for choice at all?

---

## Part 1 — Robber, Thief, Ordinary Person, Righteous: A Spectrum, Not a Character

Rav Laitman lays out four states purely as a function of the will to receive's size and correction:

| State | Description |
|---|---|
| **Robber** | Will to receive is so strong that nothing — not even being seen — stops the person from taking. |
| **Thief** | Will to receive is somewhat corrected; the person only refrains when he might be seen. |
| **Ordinary Person** | Uses the will to receive only as much as others/society permit — a socially bounded self-interest. |
| **Righteous** | The will to receive is corrected enough that the person begins actively considering how to be good with, and bestow to, others. |

**Crucial point:** none of this is a fixed character trait. It is entirely determined, moment to moment, by whatever magnitude and correction of will to receive is currently "revealed" in a person — which itself comes from above. A person cannot, in the same instant, be committing a self-interested act and also genuinely turning to the Creator; these are simply different states, following one another according to what the will to receive determines in that moment.

---

## Part 2 — The Mother's Hormone Injections: A Formative Memory

Rav Laitman shares a personal story: as a boy of twelve or thirteen, he learned that his mother, a physician, gave hormone injections that could make a man "fall in love." This unsettled him deeply — the idea that something as seemingly essential to selfhood as love could depend entirely on an external injection, with no injection meaning no love the next day. He connects this directly to the lecture's claim: **desire itself, in this framework, works exactly this way** — a person's revealed will to receive in any given moment is not a fixed personal essence but something that changes according to forces outside his control.

---

## Part 3 — Can a Thief Ask for Forgiveness Mid-Theft?

### Not Simultaneously — But Sequentially

A student asks whether a person committing theft can, in that same moment, turn to the Creator for correction. Rav Laitman is clear: **no — not at the same instant.** A person acts entirely according to whatever will to receive is revealed in him at that moment; a minute later, the will to receive may shift, and remorse and turning to the Creator become possible. States replace each other; they don't blend.

### Where the Society Comes In

Asked how a person can then ever change, Rav Laitman answers: **through the society.** Left alone, a person justifies his own self-interested actions to himself — "I have coverage for what I'm doing." Investment in the group awakens criticism and a felt deficiency for correction, and this very deficiency — the gap between the desire a person acquires from the environment and what he currently has — **is itself the prayer.** The larger this gap (the "delta"), the more surrounding light a person can draw, and the greater the correction that follows.

> *"The deficiency is between the two desires... if I capture a greater illumination of surrounding light, then I corrected my desire to a greater measure."*

---

## Part 4 — Where Is "Myself" in All of This?

### The Baby Analogy

A student presses further: if everything — including the desire to turn to the Creator — comes from the Creator, where is there any "self" left to scrutinize anything? Rav Laitman's answer draws on an analogy: a newborn taken from its mother is not yet treated as an independent entity with its own personality — it is cared for as what it currently is. **A person before receiving a screen is in an analogous position: there is no independent "I" yet to locate**, because independence itself requires a screen — a means of separating from the Creator's direct governance.

### Independence Means Working Against One's Given Nature

Rav Laitman clarifies that "independence," in this system, specifically means the capacity to act against the nature the Creator built into a person — that is, to bestow rather than simply receive for oneself. Even reaching for "receiving in order to bestow" is described as, in a sense, more radical than either theft or robbery, because it constitutes a person positioning himself as genuinely independent, "one Partzuf opposite another," rather than simply an extension of the Creator's will.

### Revisiting Still, Vegetative, Animate, Speaking

Rav Laitman returns to the four-level framework from Shamati (also discussed in the July 1 lesson) to locate where independence actually becomes possible:

- **Still:** no independent authority at all — purely fulfills the host's will.
- **Vegetative:** a little independent action against the host's will, but still fully enslaved to the norms of the surrounding environment (like plants sharing one collective life-form).
- **Animate:** greater independence, distinct individual quality, not enslaved to environment — but no genuine feeling for others.
- **Speaking:** works against the host's given nature, is not bound to environment, **and** feels others — enabling care for others and reach into past and future, not just the present.

**Why this matters for the "where is my self" question:** the more independent a person becomes (moving up this ladder), the more he is, paradoxically, positioned "against" the Creator's original nature — which is precisely the goal, since true resemblance to the Creator requires this kind of independence, even though the capacity for it is itself a gift from above.

### The Practical Answer: Invest Without Understanding

Asked directly where a person finds the intellect or strength to invest in the society before any of this is felt or understood, Rav Laitman's answer is unambiguous: **it is given from above as a starting drive; the rest depends on trying anyway** — "whatever is in your hand and power to do, that do" — even without understanding, feeling, or full intellectual grasp of why. This is the actual meaning of "the time of preparation": acting like a child learning to walk, before independence is really possible.

## Part 5 — How Does Light Actually Correct the Vessel?

Asked to explain the mechanism, Rav Laitman is candid that the exact operation cannot be described directly — only its principle, drawn from the Four Phases of Direct Light: the light that shines doesn't merely bring pleasure, it carries the very **quality of the Giver.** As a person begins to feel the one who is the source of his desire and its content, a corresponding desire to resemble that source begins to form. Correction, in this account, tracks directly with degree of revelation: **double concealment, ordinary concealment, ordinary revelation, and eternal revelation** correspond respectively to greater wickedness and greater righteousness.

### A Fully Deterministic Chain

Rav Laitman describes the process as strict cause and effect: each state (light acting on desire) begets the next state (an improved desire), which the light then acts on again, in a cascading sequence. **The desire itself cannot originate anything on its own** — it is always the light's action on the current desire that produces the next state. Whether a person actually commits an act like theft is, in this account, secondary to the underlying determination already made in the desire itself by the light.

### So Is Everything Passive?

A student asks pointedly whether this means everything — including one's own scrutiny and understanding — is passive. Rav Laitman confirms: even the capacity to make discernments, to reflect on one's own state, is itself a result of the measure of light received. What remains as "work," in this framework, is **sensitivity** — cultivating, through investment in the society, an increasing responsiveness to the light, even without being able to explain how that responsiveness itself arises.

## Key Themes & Takeaways

1. **A person's moral state (robber, thief, ordinary, righteous) is entirely a function of the magnitude and correction of the will to receive currently revealed in him** — not a fixed character trait.
2. **States do not mix.** A person cannot commit a self-interested act and turn to the Creator in the same instant; each moment is wholly determined by whatever will to receive is active then.
3. **The deficiency (the "delta") between what the society awakens in a person and his current desire is itself the prayer** — and the size of this gap determines how much surrounding light can be drawn.
4. **There is no independent "self" to locate before a screen is acquired** — like a newborn, a person before this point is properly understood as an outcome of processes acting on him, not yet an independent entity.
5. **True independence means the capacity to act against one's own given nature** — moving from Still through Vegetative and Animate to Speaking, culminating in feeling for others and reach beyond the present moment.
6. **The entire process is described as strictly deterministic** — light acting on desire produces the next state of desire, in an unbroken cascade; even scrutiny and understanding are themselves products of this process.
7. **The one thing genuinely asked of a person is sensitivity, cultivated through investment in the group** — trying without full understanding, "like a baby," in the hope of awakening a real change in desire.

## Key Terms & Concepts

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Robber / Thief / Ordinary Person / Righteous** | A spectrum determined purely by the magnitude and degree of correction of the will to receive currently revealed in a person — not fixed character types. |
| **The Delta (Gap Between Desires)** | The felt difference between a desire awakened by the society and one's current desire; this gap is itself described as the prayer. |
| **Surrounding Light (Ohr Makif)** | The illumination drawn in proportion to the size of the delta; captured more fully, it produces greater correction of desire. |
| **Screen (Masach)** | The means of separating from the Creator's direct governance; a prerequisite for any genuine independence or "self" to exist. |
| **Still / Vegetative / Animate / Speaking** | Four escalating degrees of independence from the given nature, culminating in the capacity to feel for others and draw on past and future. |
| **Double / Ordinary Concealment, Ordinary / Eternal Revelation** | Degrees of concealment and revelation that correspond respectively to greater wickedness or greater righteousness. |
| **Deterministic Cascade** | The description of spiritual development as light acting on desire to produce the next state of desire, in an unbroken, cause-and-effect sequence. |
| **Sensitivity** | The one thing described as genuinely within a person's reach to cultivate — responsiveness to the light, developed through investment in the group. |
