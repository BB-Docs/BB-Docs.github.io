### Opening

This session continues an ongoing technical course on the structure of the spiritual worlds — restriction, screen, reflected light, and the cascade from the world of Ein Sof down through Adam Kadmon and the worlds of Atzilut, Beriah, Yetzirah, and Assiya. Today's focus is a single, central concept: **Ohr Makif, surrounding light** — the light that is destined to fill a person but is currently held back, and which is the actual engine pulling a person from one degree to the next.

---

## Part 1 — Why Creation Only Receives "a Thin Line"

### The Problem with Receiving in Order to Bestow

Rav Laitman explains that after restriction, Malchut can receive light only "in order to bestow" — and the pleasure available through full adhesion with the Creator is described as 620 times greater than the basic pleasure of existence. This scale of pleasure is precisely the problem: Malchut cannot yet build a screen capable of receiving that much pleasure while still aiming entirely to bestow. **She can only take a very small, thin line of it.**

### Why the Worlds Descend

Each successive Partzuf (Galgalta, Av, Sag, Ma, Ben, and then the worlds of Atzilut, Beriah, Yetzirah, Assiya) is smaller and coarser than the one before. Rav Laitman explains this descent as the direct result of each stage discovering, more and more precisely, **how incapable it currently is** of receiving the pleasure of adhesion with the Creator while remaining oriented toward bestowal. This growing recognition of incapacity — not any lack of pleasure on the Creator's side — is what causes the cascading descent of worlds.

> *"It is the recognition of how much the greatness of the Creator is six hundred and twenty times greater than what I'm capable of being in."*

## Part 2 — What Surrounding Light (Ohr Makif) Actually Is

### Two Meanings, from Baal HaSulam

Reading from the Preface to the Wisdom of Kabbalah and Talmud Eser Sefirot, the class establishes that surrounding light is light destined to clothe a vessel but currently held back by a boundary in that vessel. Baal HaSulam gives it two senses:

1. **Distant illumination** — light that does not yet fill the vessel, but is present and real, shining from outside.
2. **Certain illumination** — light that surrounds "from all sides," giving the vessel no way to escape it until the vessel becomes fit to receive it in full.

### No Absence or Change in Spirituality

A governing principle: whatever light was originally destined to fill a vessel is never actually withdrawn — "there is no absence or change in the spiritual." What a screen rejects doesn't disappear; it becomes surrounding light, circling the vessel and shining on it from outside until the vessel is ready. This is why the light that could not fill Malchut of Ein Sof directly is guaranteed, eventually, to be received — the whole cascade of worlds exists to prepare vessels capable of receiving it.

## Part 3 — Q&A: Where Does Surrounding Light Actually Operate?

### Is Surrounding Light Only in the Point in the Heart?

A student asks whether Ohr Makif applies only to the individual point in the heart or to all of creation. Rav Laitman redirects to the larger structure first: surrounding light exists throughout the entire system, from the original desire to benefit creation through every stage of the cascading worlds — not a phenomenon limited to personal spiritual awakening alone.

### Descending vs. Ascending: Two Different Directions of Filling

Answering a question about how the worlds relate to the thin line, Rav Laitman distinguishes two directions of the same system: **from above downward**, the cascade of worlds is essentially a "thin line" — a measured concealment built specifically to allow created beings a place to exist and something to bestow toward. **From below upward**, by contrast, is where "the entire volume of Malchut of Ein Sof is filled" — each created being, by climbing degree by degree, fills its own unique place and contributes to the whole. Surrounding light is precisely what pulls a person upward through this ascent, since each new attainment reveals both what was received and — crucially — how much still remains unreceived.

### Reforming Light vs. Surrounding Light vs. Reflected Light

Asked to distinguish these, Rav Laitman clarifies: the **reforming light** is the force that actually enters and changes a person's operating nature, allowing reflected light (the intention to bestow) to develop in the first place. Group, teachers, and books exist to give a person a place to train and draw this reforming light; once a screen and reflected light are actually built, a person becomes a genuine spiritual "Partzuf" and moves to a different, less concealed level of work.

## Part 4 — Q&A: Practical Life Questions

### When Corporeal Pleasures Increase Instead of Connection

A student asks what it means when the desire for corporeal pleasure grows rather than the feeling of connection to the Creator. Rav Laitman's answer: this is not a sign that spiritual pleasures are growing elsewhere — it's a sign of **insufficient appreciation of the Creator's greatness**, and the remedy is not to fight the corporeal pull directly but to increase connection within the group (the "ten").

### What Does "Setting Boundaries in the Ten" Actually Mean?

Asked for a practical example, Rav Laitman answers simply: **"distance yourself from evil and do good."** Concretely, this means not forcing one's own judgment of "good" onto everyone else, but instead focusing on being more of a giver — bringing importance of the goal to the friends, rather than dictating to them.

### The Difference Between the Worlds

Asked to clarify the difference between the cascading worlds (Adam Kadmon, Atzilut, Beriah, Yetzirah, Assiya) and the internal lights (Nefesh, Ruach, Neshamah, Chaya, Yechida), Rav Laitman frames it as a single ladder: each world is more external or internal relative to the purpose of creation, and advancement means climbing rungs from externality to internality — becoming familiar with each degree only as one is actually prepared to climb it.

## Key Themes & Takeaways

1. **Creation can only receive light "in a thin line" at first** — because the pleasure of full adhesion with the Creator vastly exceeds what an uncorrected vessel can hold while still aiming to bestow.
2. **The descent of the spiritual worlds reflects growing self-recognition, not a shortage of divine pleasure** — each stage discovers more precisely how incapable it currently is of receiving adhesion while bestowing.
3. **Surrounding light is never actually lost or withdrawn** — whatever cannot yet be received simply surrounds the vessel from outside, guaranteed eventually to be received in full.
4. **The system works in two directions at once** — a "thin line" cascading from above, and a full filling of Malchut of Ein Sof being built from below, degree by degree, through every created being's unique contribution.
5. **Reforming light, surrounding light, and reflected light are distinct**: reforming light changes a person's basic nature; surrounding light pulls a person forward by revealing what remains unattained; reflected light is the intention to bestow that a corrected vessel develops.
6. **Growing corporeal desire, instead of spiritual connection, signals a need for greater appreciation of the Creator's greatness** — best addressed through deeper group connection, not direct suppression.
7. **Setting boundaries in the group means distancing from imposing one's own judgment and instead giving more** — practical humility rather than enforcement of personal standards.

## Key Terms & Concepts

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Ohr Makif (Surrounding Light)** | Light destined to fill a vessel but currently held back by the vessel's own limits; shines from outside and is what pulls a person to the next degree. |
| **Thin Line (Kav Dak)** | The limited measure of light a vessel can currently receive "in order to bestow," compared to the full light available. |
| **Reforming Light (Ohr Makif applied practically)** | The force that enters and changes a person's basic operating nature, enabling development of reflected light and a genuine intention to bestow. |
| **Reflected Light (Ohr Chozer)** | The intention to bestow, developed through the screen's rejection of direct pleasure, that clothes and receives the direct light appropriately. |
| **Malchut of Ein Sof** | The original, fully filled vessel of creation; the eventual goal is for created beings to fill this entire vessel from below, not merely receive a thin line from above. |
| **No Absence or Change (Ein Shinui VeHaadarah)** | The principle that light originally destined for a vessel is never truly lost — only delayed, circling as surrounding light until the vessel is ready. |
| **Adam Kadmon / Atzilut / Beriah / Yetzirah / Assiya** | The cascading worlds from most internal (closest to the purpose of creation) to most external, each with progressively coarser vessels. |
| **Setting Boundaries in the Group** | Practically defined as distancing from imposing one's own judgment on others and instead focusing on giving — bringing importance of the goal to the friends. |
