### Opening

After the group reads Rabash's article on the Zohar's definitions of a complete righteous, a complete wicked, and an intermediate, Rav Laitman turns to a lesson recorded June 23, 2002. He reframes the article's terms as a live, practical problem: the will to receive is endlessly sophisticated and keeps "running toward" spiritual pleasure even while, intellectually, a person comes to understand that this very desire must be overturned. Neither shrinking that desire, as ascetic traditions try to do, nor simply understanding the problem intellectually, can touch it. The lecture and Q&A trace how correction actually happens — through the surrounding light alone — why concealment has to hide both the depth of the vessel's corruption and the intensity of the available light, and how the article's opening categories of "complete righteous" and "complete wicked" turn out to describe a state repeated on every single degree of growth, not a fixed type of person.

---

## Part 1 — Existence from Absence and the Path of 125 Degrees

### The Will to Receive Is Built From Above, Not Chosen From Below

Rav Laitman opens the lecture with the will to receive as "existence from absence" — a desire the light itself creates and constantly develops, moving it "from above downwards" toward this world. The light does not reveal itself to this desire directly; it discloses only its "posterior" — an absence rather than a presence — which is precisely what evokes in the desire to receive a longing for fillings, and gradually teaches it that fillings require corrections.

### 125 Degrees, Five Worlds

The descent is structured as 125 degrees of concealment and diminishing light: five worlds, each divided into five partzufim (five sefirot), producing the full ladder from the wholeness of Ein Sof down to the darkness of this world. According to a person's exertion in study, the **surrounding light** — not felt directly, only illuminating "from afar" because of the disparity of form between the desire and the light — gradually corrects the kli until it can hold an intention to bestow.

> There's no other means to correct the vessel... only in awakening the surrounding light.

---

## Part 2 — Why Only the Surrounding Light Can Correct the Vessel

### An Inverse Relationship Between Light and Vessel

A person positioned just below the barrier who begins to yearn must accumulate a certain "sum of labor" before the light illuminates enough to let him cross. But a person who is stiff-necked — who ignores what the light is awakening in him and instead trusts his own will to receive to find improvement — receives less illumination, descends further from the barrier, and falls into deeper concealment. Rav Laitman describes this as a closed circle: less light produces less understanding of the problem, which produces more trouble, which requires outside pressure to break.

### Equivalence of Form Decides What Is Felt

Everything that happens does so "in accordance with equivalence of form": to the degree a person's qualities match the light's, he feels light; to the degree they don't, he feels the posterior — darkness. This is why Rav Laitman urges the group to actively examine which thoughts, actions, and states most efficiently invite the surrounding light, rather than exerting effort without checking whether it's producing results.

## Part 3 — The Contradiction: A Will to Receive That Knows It Will Be Overturned, Yet Still Runs Toward It

### The Student's Question

A student asks how to reconcile two facts about the will to receive: it is extraordinarily sophisticated, always finding new openings — yet at the same time it intellectually understands that the entire path leads to its own correction ("death"), and still runs forward as if it doesn't understand.

### Growth of Desire Is Not the Same as Progress

Rav Laitman answers that what looks like improvement as a person advances — from laborer, to businessman, to scientist — is really just a bigger desire to receive, not a change in kind. The felt sense of "getting better" is simply the desire enlarging and aiming itself, however imperfectly, toward the Creator.

### Comparing Methods for Handling the Desire

| Approach | Aim Regarding the Desire to Receive | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Ascetic practice (e.g., the lotus posture) | Diminish the desire toward zero | A kind of numbed calm ("nirvana") achieved by deadening feeling, not correcting it |
| Insular or "fanatic" religious life | Shrink personal desire, submerge it in group certainty | Real relief from suffering, but through diminishment rather than correction of intention |
| The Kabbalistic method | Let the desire keep growing; do nothing to it directly | The desire is aimed entirely at the Creator by drawing the surrounding light, which alone reshapes the intention |

> Forget about how you are. Don't think about your own qualities. Stay with them. Be concerned about the light that will come to you.

Asked directly why a person needs to justify to himself the "energy" it costs to give without benefit, Rav Laitman agrees the contradiction is real and lives inside every person — the will to receive still wants reward even while trying to work in order to bestow — and says this tension itself is meant to be brought, unresolved, into the demand for correction, not argued away.

## Part 4 — Why Concealment Is Necessary On Both Sides

### What Would Happen Without It

If there were no concealment, Rav Laitman explains, the will to receive would either chase spiritual pleasure directly and simply become another klipa (shell) working for itself, or — if it truly understood that it was going to be "killed" — it would refuse to cooperate at all. Concealment protects the process from both directions: from the side of the vessel, a person would be too afraid to touch such an enormous will to receive; from the side of the light, the intensity of the pleasures available would overwhelm a person entirely.

> They hide away from you your vessels and they hide the pleasures... and they hide the lights away from you because if you discover what lights are there... you will have no room to think of anything else. You'll be locked on it like a madman. This is called Pulsa de Nura, a strike of light.

### Lishma Cannot Be Reached by Labor Alone

Citing Rabash on the matter of Lishma (working for the Creator's sake), Rav Laitman states plainly that no counsel or tactic can produce it — "I labored and I found" means labor produces the deficiency, not the attainment itself. Citing the Mishnah's "it is not upon you to finish the work, but you are not free to idle away from it," he explains that a person's task is to build the deficiency (prayer), and the filling — Lishma — arrives only as a gift from above, in response.

## Part 5 — Light, Vessel, and the Shattering: Where the Surrounding Light Comes From

### Two Things Only: Creator and Created Being

Rav Laitman reduces the entire structure to two elements: the feeling of the Creator's presence inside the vessel is called **light**; its absence is called **darkness** or **the lack**. Through the four phases of direct light, the will to receive was originally filled to the state of **Malchut of Ein Sof**.

### The Shattering and What Remains "Surrounding"

Before the descent, the general soul (Adam HaRishon) existed in Atzilut in complete unity with the Creator — "He and His Name are One." The shattering (the sin of the Tree of Knowledge) broke that soul into many parts, which fell to this world, while the light that had filled it remained above, in Atzilut. Relative to the fallen souls, that same light — once internal — is now called **surrounding light**: present, but not yet received, because the vessels below are not yet corrected to hold it.

### Correction Is Gradual, By Design

Correcting the soul to bestow, degree by degree, is what allows that light to fill a person incrementally — the full 125 degrees represent 125 such steps of correction matched to 125 measures of filling.

---

## Part 6 — Studying the Creator's Actions in Order to Adhere to Him

### Imitating the Action, Not Just Understanding It

Asked to clarify what it means to "be like Him," Rav Laitman explains that a person reaches resemblance to the Creator not by intellectually grasping His qualities, but by observing his own corrupted states, discerning how they should have been corrected, and then wanting, through that same corrective action, to be joined with the Creator in the deed itself — adhesion, not mere imitation.

### All Actions Are Actions of Desire

He stresses that "spiritual actions" are not physical acts of hands and legs but actions within the desire itself — the soul's 620 discernible parts, each a particular manner of wanting to enjoy. The work is to use each desire with the intention of resembling and adhering to the Creator, not to suppress or replace the desire itself.

---

## Part 7 — Complete Righteous and Complete Wicked on Every Degree

### Returning to the Article's Terms

Rav Laitman ties the discussion back to the article directly: on every degree, without exception, a person passes through a state of "complete wicked" and a state of "complete righteous." Before beginning a degree, in the darkness of the left line, a person determines for himself — no one else can determine it for him — that he is completely wicked, that everything in him is oriented toward receiving. Later, on the right line, correcting and feeling a measure of spirituality, he becomes, on that same degree, "complete righteous."

### Why "Complete" Doesn't Mean the Whole Soul

"Complete" refers only to whatever percentage of the soul's desires are currently revealed — perhaps 10% at a given stage. If a person recognizes the full corruption of that 10% and corrects it, he is complete righteous with respect to it. When an additional 1% is revealed, it is not simply added on: from the vantage point of that larger awareness, the previous 10% is now seen as corrupted too, so the deeper a person advances, the worse his own prior states appear in retrospect — "now we have 11 bad degrees."

### Why This, Too, Requires Concealment

This retrospective worsening is why, Rav Laitman says, the path cannot be shown to a person in advance: seeing in full how terrible each successive degree will look would be unbearable. The same concealment that hides the depth of the vessel's corruption also protects a person from that despair.

## Part 8 — Closing Round: What the Group Takes From the Lesson

In the closing sharing, students name the practical thread they are taking from the lesson: cultivating a real deficiency rather than a wish to be filled, using the group and its shared demand to sharpen and clarify that request, and recognizing that equivalence of form below the barrier is reached only by inviting the surrounding light — never by direct action on the desire to receive itself. Rav Laitman closes by returning to the article's own note about preparing to receive abundance (purity, blessing, "the table before the Lord") and restates the aim of all of it as adhesion with the Creator — "the return of Israel to the Lord your God."

> If you feel bad, open a book. That's it. Not more.

## Key Themes & Takeaways

1. **The will to receive is built "existence from absence" by the light itself**, descending through 125 degrees across five worlds before reaching the darkness of this world — concealment is built into the design, not an obstacle to it.
2. **Only the surrounding light can correct the vessel below the barrier.** No direct action on the desire to receive — enlarging it, shrinking it, or reasoning with it — can touch the intention; only inviting the light changes it.
3. **Growth of desire is not the same as spiritual progress.** What feels like advancement (from laborer to scientist) is often just a larger desire to receive, still aimed at self-benefit.
4. **The Kabbalistic method differs from ascetic and "fanatic" approaches** by refusing to diminish the desire; instead, the desire is left to grow and is aimed, through the surrounding light, entirely toward the Creator.
5. **Concealment protects from both directions** — from the vessel's fear of an enormous, ungoverned will to receive, and from being overwhelmed ("driven mad") by the intensity of the light if it were revealed too soon.
6. **Lishma cannot be produced by labor** — labor produces the deficiency (prayer); the filling itself is a gift from above, not a direct result of personal effort.
7. **"Complete righteous" and "complete wicked" are not fixed identities** but states repeated on every degree, defined relative to whatever percentage of the soul's desires is currently revealed — and each new degree retroactively exposes the previous one as corrupted too.

## Key Terms & Concepts

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Existence from Absence** | The will to receive as created directly by the light, out of nothing resembling the light's own nature — as opposed to the light itself, which is "existence from existence." |
| **Surrounding Light (Ohr Makif)** | Light that has not yet entered the vessel because of disparity of form; it illuminates "from afar" and is the only force capable of correcting the will to receive below the barrier. |
| **125 Degrees** | The full ladder of correction — five worlds, each with five partzufim/sefirot — spanning the descent from Ein Sof to this world and the corresponding path back up. |
| **Malchut of Ein Sof** | The state in which the will to receive was originally filled completely by the Creator's light, prior to the shattering. |
| **The Shattering** | The breaking of the unified soul of Adam HaRishon (the "sin of the Tree of Knowledge") into many parts, which fell to this world while the light that had filled the soul remained above in Atzilut. |
| **Equivalence of Form** | The governing law determining what a person feels: to the extent his qualities match the light's, he feels light; to the extent they diverge, he feels darkness (the light's "posterior"). |
| **Lishma** | Working "for Her sake" — for the Creator's sake, not one's own; an illumination from above that cannot be produced by labor or tactics, only prayed for. |
| **"I Labored and I Found"** | The principle that personal effort produces the deficiency (the prayer) but not the attainment itself, which arrives as a gift in response. |
| **Klipa / Sitra Achra** | The "shell" or "other side" — the orientation of wanting to receive for oneself alone, which a person must recognize and hand a portion to (symbolically) even after a corrected meal. |
| **Adhesion (Dvekut)** | The ultimate goal of the work — not merely imitating the Creator's qualities intellectually, but joining Him in the very deed, becoming like Him through repeated correction. |
| **Complete Righteous / Complete Wicked** | States (not fixed types) that recur on every degree, defined relative to the percentage of the soul's desires currently revealed to a person. |
| **Pulsa de Nura** | "A strike of light" — the danger of being overwhelmed by revealed spiritual pleasure before a person is prepared to receive it, one of the reasons concealment is necessary. |
