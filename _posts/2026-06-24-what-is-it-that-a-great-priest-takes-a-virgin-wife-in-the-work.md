### Opening

The lesson takes up an article that reads the Torah's laws about marriage, priesthood, and forbidden unions as a coded description of the soul's development. The central question: why does the Torah's language of women, marriage, and prohibited unions map onto the inner stages a soul passes through — and what does it mean, concretely, for a person's will to receive to be "married" to a screen?

---

## Part 1 — Reality Has Only Two Parts

Rav Laitman opens with a foundational claim: reality consists of only two things — the Creator and the created being. Everything else that looks like separate parts of reality, in both corporeality and spirituality, has no independent existence. Just as our world divides into the still, vegetative, animate, and speaking (with the speaking, man, as the most developed level), spirituality has the same four levels, with the "speaking" soul governing the rest.

### Four Phases, Four Desires

The will to receive itself develops through four phases (paralleling the four phases of direct light): still, vegetative, animate, and speaking desire, each greater than the last. Only the "speaking" level is independent — detached from the Creator, feeling its own lack, with the Creator as the source of pleasure toward it. The measure to which the Creator "clothes" in the created being is called the measure of life or livelihood.

---

## Part 2 — Women as States of Soul Development

### Why the Gemara Discusses Women So Extensively

Rav Laitman explains that the Gemara's extensive treatment of women — married, divorced, a bride, and so on — is not really about women at all, but about internal states the soul passes through in its development. He references material describing roughly seventy stages of a woman's development from young to mature, and notes that everything in this world is simply a projection, a "shadow," of processes in the spiritual world. Nothing is genuinely new in this world; all innovation happens above and is copied down.

### Man and Woman Within a Single Person

Both a man and a woman, in this reading, exist within a single person: **woman represents the will to receive; man represents the will to bestow.** The capacity to bestow — the screen — is called *Gever* (man), and develops from the point in the heart onward. Every act of overcoming (the screen) has, opposite it, the will to receive (Nukva) that it works upon. Different spiritual states require different corrected desires — which is why the article's laws about who a priest may or may not marry map onto rules about which desires can be worked with at a given spiritual degree, and which cannot yet be corrected.

> *"Whatever we have in our world, whatever we can discern... between man and woman, is a very small part of what takes place in spirituality."*

## Part 3 — Q&A: Sensing the Stages, Time of Preparation

### Can a Person Recognize These States Before the Machsom?

A student asks whether, even during the time of preparation, a person can begin to sense these stages. Rav Laitman answers that the time of preparation is precisely a period of drawing greater surrounding light through effort — a person feels both his intolerable state against his own will to receive and, simultaneously, a growing attraction to spirituality. He compares it to being able to roughly make out shapes in half-darkness: a person cannot yet fully identify these states, but can begin to sense their outline.

### Once Past the Machsom, Does a Person Stop Caring About Worldly Detail?

Rav Laitman observes that a person who has attained spirituality can, in principle, describe the corporeal processes of this world with great precision — but does not get absorbed in that detail; he may even appear naive about ordinary worldly calculations precisely because his attention is elsewhere.

### What Is the Machsom, Precisely?

Asked whether these stages can be reached before the machsom (the barrier), Rav Laitman states that the machsom is itself the first spiritual degree — the point past which a person no longer returns fully to this world, since his upper limit of attainment is now already in spirituality. An "unmarried" state in spirituality is impossible in the article's terms: a corrected will to receive is called "a woman," and one must go through the stages of correcting the will to receive (infancy, youth, maturity) until it can be "married" — joined through a common screen (the *chuppah*) — and coupled with the upper light.

## Part 4 — Spiritual Action and Corporeal Life

### Does Spiritual Progress Have to Show Up in One's Corporeal Marriage?

A student asks whether attaining these inner stages relates to, or should influence, his actual corporeal marriage. Rav Laitman affirms there is a real connection — spiritual and corporeal development clothe one in the other — and describes practical steps their own group takes to involve the women of the community (a weekly lesson, mutual activities), while noting a woman is "not obliged" to participate the same way, though it helps.

He adds a broader observation: the male principle, in truth, belongs to the Creator and descends specifically to correct the will to receive; even an unmarried man is nonetheless "obliged," in a sense, by an inner "womanly" deficiency that drives him to seek and to build in the world — everything, he notes, exists to serve something else in this framework.

### No Habits in Spirituality

A distinctive point raised: in corporeality, repetition becomes habit — a person gets used to something and stops tasting it. In spirituality, by contrast, the desire to receive is renewed constantly, so every state is genuinely new: new attainment, new work, new "flavor," each time.

---

## Part 5 — Kingdom of Heaven and the Final Correction

### Recognizing the Kingdom of Heaven

Rav Laitman describes the "Kingdom of Heaven" (Malchut of heaven) as the state in which a person recognizes, with all his desires, thoughts, and awareness, that the Creator controls everything — a recognition given as a starting point ("existence from absence") from which the rest is gradually built. The relationship to this recognition can run from total resistance (illustrated by Pharaoh: "who is the Creator, that I should listen to Him?") to full, willing inclusion — "like the horse and rider going together."

### What Changes at the Final Correction

Asked how the final correction differs from present reality, Rav Laitman is cautious: it cannot really be described in advance, because at that stage every desire receives an entirely new vessel and is experienced in order to bestow rather than in order to receive — a genuinely different way of seeing, not simply a change of circumstance. He references an article on the "last generation" as a partial, still-incomplete attempt to describe it: the inner law of "love your friend as yourself" becomes each person's actual internal drive, so that people compete to give their maximum for the collective rather than to take the maximum for themselves.

## Key Themes & Takeaways

1. **Reality consists only of the Creator and the created being** — everything that looks like separate parts of reality, on either side, is a functional description, not an independent thing.
2. **The Gemara's language of women, marriage, and forbidden unions maps onto internal stages of the soul's development** — not literal instructions about women, but a technical language for a person's evolving relationship between will to receive and screen.
3. **Man (bestowal/screen) and woman (will to receive) both exist within a single person**; correction is the ongoing work of "marrying" desire to a screen appropriate to a person's current spiritual degree.
4. **The machsom is the first actual spiritual degree** — the point after which a person's true "address" is no longer fully in this world.
5. **Spirituality has no habituation.** Every renewed desire is genuinely new, unlike the repetition and dulling that characterizes corporeal habit.
6. **Recognition of the "Kingdom of Heaven" is a given starting point**, and a person's stance toward it can range from total resistance to full, willing inclusion.
7. **The final correction cannot be fully described in advance** — it involves a wholly new vessel and mode of perception, not merely improved circumstances; even partial descriptions (e.g., of the "last generation") remain incomplete.

## Key Terms & Concepts

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Four Phases (of Direct Light)** | The developmental stages — still, vegetative, animate, speaking — through which the will to receive itself develops; only the "speaking" level is independent. |
| **Woman / Nukva** | In the Work, the will to receive, or a corrected desire ready to be worked with; the Gemara's varied descriptions of women correspond to stages of this desire's development. |
| **Man / Gever** | The will to bestow; the screen, developing from the point in the heart, that overcomes and works with the will to receive. |
| **Screen (Masach)** | The corrective faculty that allows a person to receive in order to bestow; central to the marriage metaphor as what "weds" a desire into corrected use. |
| **Chuppah** | In the metaphor, the general screen uniting desire and correction, prerequisite to a "coupling" (zivug) with the upper light. |
| **Machsom (Barrier)** | The first true spiritual degree; past it, a person's primary attainment is no longer located in this world. |
| **Kingdom of Heaven (Malchut of Heaven)** | The recognition that the Creator controls all of a person's desires, thoughts, and awareness; given as a starting point, developed through one's stance toward it. |
| **Final Correction (Gmar Tikkun)** | The completed state in which every desire receives a new vessel oriented entirely toward bestowal; cannot be fully described from within the current state. |
| **Time of Preparation** | The period before the machsom in which surrounding light draws a person forward through effort, producing a growing (if still indistinct) attraction to spirituality. |
